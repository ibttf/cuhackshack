Hack Shack 2025

I'm Roy (Columbia '27, Z Fellow). I built Interview Coder, cheatware for techincal interviews that hit 5-figure MRR in two weeks and generated 20M+ impressions across social media.

Columbia has a bunch of talented undergraduate builders, but they're scattered across campus. During my time here, me and my cofounder Neel have spent a lot of time looking for them.

After meeting around a thousand students and talking with hundreds, we’ve picked 12 of the highest-signal people we’ve met for a weekend-long retreat: Hack Shack (inspired by Hack Lodge).

The Event

From February 28 to March 2, 2025, we're bringing together Columbia's most high-signal founders, engineers, and builders for an invite-only hacker retreat of deep work, collaboration, and friend-making.

This is not a hackathon. It's a closed-room gathering of the most promising undergraduate builders we've met at this school.

Some of the people joining:

  • Columbia '27 student who's raised $1.5M to compete with Claude computer use.
  • Columbia '25 student (valedictorian of the engineering school) building a Perplexity competitor.
  • Columbia' 26 (on leave) developing a self-administered test to detect deadly contaminants in drinks and drugs.
  • A high schooler (Columbia '29) who built AI-powered chess glasses that went super viral.

We've already gathered an amazing group, but we might have space for one or two more. If you're a Columbia undergraduate working on something ambitious and want to build alongside Columbia's best, DM me on X or find me or Neel on campus.
